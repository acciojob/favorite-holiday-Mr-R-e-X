GOA

Spread over an area of 3702 Sq. Kms., and a maximum altitude of 1022 metres from the sea level, Goa has a tropical climate with a maximum rainfall of 130 to 140 inches between June and September. The population of Goa is around 1.4 Million.


  1. Baga Beach: Known for its vibrant nightlife, Baga Beach is one of the most popular beaches in North Goa. You can indulge in water sports, enjoy delicious seafood at beach shacks, and experience the lively atmosphere.
  2. Old Goa: Explore the rich history and architecture of Old Goa, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visit iconic churches like Basilica of Bom Jesus, Se Cathedral, and Church of St. Francis of Assisi, which showcase the region's colonial past.
  3. Dudhsagar Falls: Located on the Mandovi River, Dudhsagar Falls is a breathtaking cascade surrounded by lush greenery. You can trek through the Bhagwan Mahaveer Sanctuary to reach the falls or take a thrilling jeep safari.
  4. Anjuna Flea Market: If you love shopping, don't miss the Anjuna Flea Market. Held every Wednesday, this market offers a wide range of goods including clothing, accessories, jewelry, handicrafts, and souvenirs. It's a great place to bargain and find unique items.
  5. Aguada Fort: Situated atop a hill overlooking the Arabian Sea, Aguada Fort is a historic Portuguese fortification built in the 17th century. Explore the ramparts, lighthouse, and underground water storage chambers while enjoying panoramic views of the coastline.
